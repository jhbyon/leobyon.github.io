Bio

         

    I am a theoretical physicist working on the study of the early universe. I completed the undergrad at the University of Veracruz, in Mexico, before moving to Valencia. Here I just recently completed my PhD, under the supervision of Olga Mena, which focused on the inflationary paradigm: theory, phenomenological predictions and current constraints. I am particularly interested in the mathematical construction of well-motivated models and in the study of nonstandard observables that could help us in determining the true nature of inflation.

Research

Inflation       

Read more

    The inflationary theory is the simplest and most elegant idea to solve the main problems of the standar model of Cosmology and which also sets the initial conditions for the evolution of the quantum perturbations that evolved into the current large-scale structures in the universe.
           One of my main interests is the construction of inflationary models that reproduce the current observations from the cosmic microwave background (CMB). While the most famous constructions rely on different complicated shapes for the potential energy of the particle responsible for driving the inflationary evolution, I study the consequences of modifying the laws of Gravity during such times in order to obtain different predictions with the simplest potentials. Models constructed in these new frameworks are now being studied.
           I am also interested in studying general model-independent descriptions of inflation using approaches which go beyond the standar assumptions of the so-called slow-roll approximation. Going beyond the standar lore allows to study new scenarios of inflation, as ultra-slow-roll scenarios, and catalog models which belong to general theories of Gravity. On the other hand, the mathematical computation of the inflationary observables ― as the power spectra of scalar and tensor perturbations, the spectral indices and their own scale dependences ― usually assumes the slow-roll hierarchy and thus understanding correctly the slow-roll approaches will lead to a correct prediction for the observables.
           In general, I am interested in:

    • Well-motivated inflationary scenarios (G-inflation, alpha-attractors, warm inflation, etc.)
    • The physics of reheating
    • New probes of the physics of inflation (LSS, 21cm, etc.)
    • Multifield models
    • Gauge fields during inflation
    • Gravitational waves form inflation
    • Non-Gaussianities
    • Any viable alternative to inflation...
          

Modified     
Gravity

    The nature of the dark energy that fills around the 70% of the universe remains a mystery. Although some explanations involve a new type of fluid, the simplest solution would be a modification of General Relativity (GR) at large scales.
           In this regard, I am interested in general scalar-tensor theories of Gravity and models embedded therein which lead to second-order equations of motion ― and thus free from instabilities ― which evade Solar System tests, where GR has been proved to be a good theory. Theories of this kind such as Horndeski, have already been exploited for the study of the accelerated expansion of the Universe but also for the inflationary theory.
